## The Old Man

> The birds led the party into the temple district of Domusirex and into a room where an old man waited with a girl at his side. He offered to explain why Nith M'aera had been sealed away.

## The Lore He Gave

> He gestured, and the room shimmered into illusion to match his words.

>> 1. **Nith M'aera** had once been a devout follower of the Seven. In exchange for her devotion she had been granted power, and at first she had used it to carry out their wills in towns and temples like any other servant.
>> 2. She had not stayed satisfied. She learned to **pocket the Seven's grace into trinkets**, hoarding it, until she had stored enough divinity to ascend on her own and gift power to anyone or anything she chose.
>> 3. The Seven had grown lazy. Their power was wood in a flame fed by the faith of their followers, and Nith fed her own flame the same way: monsters and devoted cultists, instilling fear in the Seven's faithful and turning their faith elsewhere.
>> 4. The old man eventually learned how to do to her what she had done to them. He pulled her grace back into trinkets and weakened her enough to challenge her in a remote part of the world. He could not end her. He could only bind her in a bubble that kept her power from leaking out.
>> 5. *"This world has been changed to inspire others to place their faith in her, and to challenge our own domains. Do not be fooled into thinking that we sealed away magic, that is her invention with the goal to sow doubt. We had only sealed her."*

## Atrix

> The old man called the girl by name and sent her to outfit the party. *"Atrix. Make sure they have what they need for their travels. Once they are on their way, find Dyrena."*

>> 1. **Atrix** bowed and addressed him as *brother*. He stepped through a portal of his own making and was gone.
>> 2. **Heron** asked, carefully, whether they too could ascend by stealing divine grace into trinkets. Atrix went nervous, then steady: the path was real, but the Seven were not careless with their power any more.
>> 3. Three of the party heard the proposal and declined the next leg of the work. **Ali**, **Waldemar**, and **Sylvanis** stepped away for reasons of their own.

## A Detective Stops By

> Atrix led the remaining two through the city. Her appearance dulled to commoner outside the College, and she stopped at a guardhouse where a short dwarven woman stood reading a case file with a hand over one eye.

>> 1. The collision was intentional on Atrix's part. The dwarf was **Varalia Kilnscorch**, a forensic investigator from the dwarven city of **Duhaaren**, a city which in the old timeline had been a mere human keep built atop a mountain instead of inside one.
>> 2. Atrix flashed green light into Varalia's eyes and gave her the same dual-memory experience the party had survived in the cave. *"I have removed Nith M'aera's influence over you."* In exchange, Atrix wanted Varalia to travel south with the party. Varalia accepted.
>> 3. Outside the guardhouse, Atrix knelt over bare earth, planted a seed, and chanted a few syllables. A vibrant gold-coloured lily bloomed within moments. Its stamen was made of jewel, its petals of solid gold. *"This should be enough to procure supplies for your journey. I would offer more, but I do not wish to draw too much attention."*

## The Week

> Days passed while the party waited for an audience that never properly arrived.

>> 1. **Varalia** gathered intelligence on the **Wanhoop Swamp** to the south: a swamp that had been expanding for decades, a village it had swallowed three years prior, rumours of a dragon, of blood-sucking leeches, of a hag who commanded a creature that could petrify a person.
>> 2. The summons to the barracks came eventually. **Captain Valerius** told the party the higher-ups had decided their reports were enough on their own. The party was free to go. The same goblin lifted the sigils from their hands.
>> 3. *"May Atrix bless your travels,"* the captain said as he shook their hands.

## Leaf

> Atrix was waiting for them outside the barracks. Varalia had decided not to come south after all. Removing Nith's influence had been heavier than expected.

>> 1. Atrix had a different plan. Outside the southern gate, she whispered to a leafy bush and shaped it into a bipedal creature of bark and rustling leaves. Small bow, stone-tipped arrows, a stone blade beneath a cloak of foliage.
>> 2. *"This is **Leaf**. They will aid you on your journey for now. I have imbued as much of my grace as I can afford into them."*
>> 3. Leaf could say *"I am Leaf"*, with various intonations, and not much else. The goddess had spent her budget on dexterity instead of intellect. Leaf demonstrated by lifting **Heron's** coin pouch off him without anyone noticing.
>> 4. Leaf's other gift was direction. They pointed south, said *"I am Leaf!"* with great confidence, and the swamp would not let them get lost on the way.
>> 5. Atrix crumbled into a pile of leaves and a western wind carried her away. *"I will watch over you all whenever I can."*
