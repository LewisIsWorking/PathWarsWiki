## Bodies to Burn

> The fires that had been set to the village had to be put out before the survivors could begin the more painful work of accounting for the dead. **Quack's** mutagens were poor at firefighting; everyone else's hands and waterskins did better.

>> 1. The party and the surviving villagers spent the rest of the day moving rubble, dousing what they could, and laying the dead in rows behind the church.
>> 2. **Wrenwick** identified his wife's body and his child's body and would not let anyone else handle them.

## The Herald's Voice

> Late in the afternoon, the body of the orc herald, still where Bereket's arrow had left it, opened its eyes.

>> 1. The eyes were not the herald's. The voice was not the herald's. **Bereket** recognised the rhythm of the words before he recognised the speaker, and went very still.
>> 2. *"Sagitari of the Tempest. You stand on a field I sent. The fire was mine. The lesson was mine. You served by standing on the other side of it."*
>> 3. **Buros**, through the corpse. The god Bereket had carried into every fight since the arena.
>> 4. *"Why,"* Bereket asked, and could not finish.
>> 5. *"Because the world is folding the wrong way. Because some places must be cleansed before the wrong faith roots. You were sent to learn what the cleansing looks like when it does not pretend to be just."*
>> 6. The corpse closed its eyes again. The body did not move further.

## A Quiet Decision

> Bereket sat on his hooves and stared at the ground for a long time. **Heron** did not interrupt. **Húxiān** sat near, not speaking, present.

>> 1. *"If he sent that,"* Bereket said at last, *"then he is not the god I bent my bow for."*
>> 2. He did not renounce Buros aloud. He did not need to.

## The Pyre

> The villagers had no priest left. Heron stood in for one. The pyre was lit at dusk.

>> 1. *"Let Dyrena's light guide them out of Vulla's shadow,"* Heron said. The villagers said it back to him in voices that did not quite hold.
>> 2. The fire burned through the night. The party kept watch in shifts, and no further raiders came.

## The White Plume

> At first light on the morning after, the smoke had cleared from the village, and a different smoke had taken its place on the horizon.

>> 1. A single column of **pale white smoke**, miles to the south, standing absolutely straight despite a clear breeze.
>> 2. Heron's pull, dim since the fight, lit again at the sight of it.
>> 3. Quack packed the kit. Húxiān said goodbye to the old woman in the church. Bereket lifted the new axe with the bow still on his back.
>> 4. The party turned south once more.
