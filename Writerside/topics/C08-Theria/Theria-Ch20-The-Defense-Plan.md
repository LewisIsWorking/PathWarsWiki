## Mapping the Ground

> The village was a cluster of about thirty buildings around a small square and a single road that ran north to south. The orchard surrounded it on three sides. A shallow stream ran east of the square. Six low stone walls between rows of trees broke up the approaches.

>> 1. **Heron** and the old man walked the perimeter together at first light on the day after the party arrived. The road was the obvious attack line. The orchard could be defended at the stone walls if the villagers were placed well, but the walls were not tall enough to hold a sustained push.
>> 2. **Bereket** picked two firing positions on the roofs of the taller buildings near the square. **Quack** sketched out where alchemical traps could be set without endangering children working the lower branches.
>> 3. **Húxiān** counted villagers and quickly stopped counting. Forty adults, perhaps fifty, of whom roughly half could be armed in any meaningful sense.

## The Old Man's Stores

> The hilltop house had a cellar the village had not known about until that morning.

>> 1. Twenty short bows, two dozen quivers of arrows, a half-rack of polearms, and a small barrel of caltrops. *"From an old habit. The village would not have liked the look of them."*
>> 2. The old man did not explain where they had come from. The polearms were old but well-kept. The bows had been strung within the last year.

## The Plan

> By noon, the plan had a shape.

>> 1. The road would be left open as a feint, with caltrops sown beneath a thin layer of hay at the southern entry.
>> 2. The first stone wall on the southern orchard would be the killing line, with Bereket and three of the village's better archers above the square.
>> 3. Heron and Quack would hold the gap between the wall and the square as a fallback. Húxiān would float between positions with the wand of healing and her short blade.
>> 4. The old man would stay at the hilltop with the children and the wounded. He had things up there, he said, that did not need to be explained.

## Waiting

> The second day passed in finishing what had been started. Traps placed. Caltrops sown. Arrows fletched. The villagers were quiet in the way people are quiet before something they have not been able to picture properly.

>> 1. The old man took Heron aside in the evening and spoke to him for an hour. Heron came back with a face that had been working on something for a long time.
>> 2. **Dura** put the small girl to bed early and sat with Quack on the porch, asking technical questions about elixirs of life.

## The Watch on the Third Morning

> Bereket spotted them first. The southern road, a column of dust, smaller than the herald's wave but tighter.

>> 1. *"They are here,"* he said, and the village began to move.
