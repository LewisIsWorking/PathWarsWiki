## The Mess Hall

> Voices ahead, casual and unguarded. The kind a cult speaks in when no one is supposed to be coming.

>> 1. **Ali** crept forward and confirmed three or four voices in conversation. Routine, not alarmed.
>> 2. The party charged together. The room was lit by sconces, a table set with bread, a third figure rummaging at a cupboard. Three cultists in robes, hoods down, caught between bites.
>> 3. The fight was over before it began. Two cultists dropped under the opening flurry. The third tried to crawl for the far door and **Waldemar** brought his maul down on the back of his skull.
>> 4. No one was left alive to question. **Bereket**: *"Dead men tell no tales."* Loot was minor: cheap wine, daggers, tableware.
>> 5. **Heron** closed each cultist's eyes and folded their arms. A short prayer: *"Let Dyrena's light guide you out of Vulla's shadow."*

## The Hallway of Doors

> Four doors. One hallway. Murmuring beyond.

>> 1. **Ali** worked each door. Two on the left, empty and untrapped. The door on the right, trapped at the top with a wedge of unknown purpose. The far door, no trap, with chanting beyond.
>> 2. The chant carried multiple voices and a methodical rhythm. Ali listened harder and judged the ritual close to its climax. Thirty minutes, at most.
>> 3. The math was harsh: the message owl would reach **Captain Kael** before the ritual completed, but reinforcements would not. Sending the message anyway was Bereket's call. If the party failed, the army would at least know where to come.
>> 4. The owl figurine's eyes flashed bright blue, the figurine became a living silver owl, and it flew out of the cave behind the party.

## The Anteroom

> The far door opened to a small damp room with three more cultists. One was already at the back wall, hood up, and bolted down a corridor the moment they saw the party.

>> 1. **Waldemar** focused the closer of the remaining two and dropped him before he could ready himself.
>> 2. **Bereket** put two arrows into the second's chest as Waldemar's strike connected.
>> 3. **Ali** flanked, **Heron** swept a leg, and the second cultist hit the floor. Waldemar's next swing was pulled at the last moment for a nonlethal blow to the head. Ali's folded fan to the face finished the work.
>> 4. One dead, one bound, one fled. The chant still ran down the corridor. The other door in the room stayed closed.

## The Barricade

> The fled cultist had warned the others, and debris had been thrown behind a door to slow the breach.

>> 1. **Waldemar** broke the door down with his maul. The work took precious minutes.
>> 2. The party stepped over the wreckage and emerged into a large cavern with a dais of unfamiliar stone, four crystals humming under their own light, and the ritual already in motion.
