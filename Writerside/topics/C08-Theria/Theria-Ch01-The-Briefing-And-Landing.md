## The Crossing

> Morning fog rolled thick as soup over the deck of the Twins' ship as it cut toward the Isle of Nith. The captain navigated by faith more than sight.

>> 1. **Warpriest Valerius Kael** called the volunteers to attention before landing. Fifty souls in ten parties of five, dispatched to break a cult before its ritual could complete.
>> 2. The target was **Nith M'aera**, the beast Lord Cumeyr put down in this very place generations ago. Cultists were attempting to resurrect her somewhere in a cave system on the island. Numbers unknown. Ritual unknown.
>> 3. Each lieutenant carried a **messenger bird token**, a small owl figurine that would deliver a single message back to Kael when activated.
>> 4. The party drew **Brother Elias**, third son of **Noble House Valor**. He served the **Divine Twins**, the paired sun and moon. Earnest, polished, and untested.

## The Party

> Four strangers found themselves bound to one purpose under Elias's command.

>> - **Heron**: priest's aide in white and blue robes over ceramic plate, scarred across the cheekbone, hooked gaff at his belt and a steel shield on his back.
>> - **Waldemar**: tall and sturdy, trimmed beard, slightly pointed ears, deep rough voice, a maul that promised concussions.
>> - **Bereket Sisay**: Sagitari of the **School of the Tempest**, formerly of **Cumeyr's Rest** arena. Lean and dark-skinned with dreadlocks framing a two-tone bow stave at his back. He swore his work in Buros's name.
>> - **Ali**: slight and quiet, twin bladed metal fans at her waist.

> The warpriest's quartermaster requisitioned each of them a potion of healing and five gold's worth of common gear before they boarded the row boats.

## The Isle

> The Isle of Nith rose from the fog as half a mile of grey shore backed by dense treeline. A hundred feet of peak jutted above the canopy.

>> 1. Three other parties peeled off into the trees during the northern march, breaking line of sight by agreement.
>> 2. Elias halted his five on the northern shore and laid out a loose comb formation, each searcher in sight of the next, the rest within shouting distance.
>> 3. Forty minutes in, **Heron caught movement**, a figure breaking cover and bolting deeper into the trees before vanishing in the canopy. Elias narrowed the search.
>> 4. Shortly after, **Bereket cut the trail**. Hardened ground from repeated use, snapped branches, and a clear set of footprints leading to a rock outcropping hidden in the foliage.
>> 5. Behind it, a hole gaped in the rock. Wide enough for a man, deep enough that the bottom couldn't be seen from the lip.

## The Mouth of the Cave

> Elias pulled out his owl trinket and stared at it for a long moment before tucking it away unactivated. He wanted confirmation that the cave wasn't empty before bringing the rest of the search party down on a wasted lead.

>> 1. **Ali** offered a torch from her pack. **Bereket** carried rope and grapple but didn't set either; the climb was simple enough.
>> 2. Elias offered a small prayer; a glowing orb of light formed at his shoulder and followed him into the dark.
>> 3. The descent ran roughly twenty feet, fifteen of climb and two of drop off the rocky wall. **Ali used the lieutenant as a landing pad** to break what would otherwise have been a worse fall. Elias caught her, flushed, and dusted himself off.
>> 4. The party gathered at the bottom of the shaft beneath the lieutenant's drifting orb, a tunnel opening into rock ahead of them.
