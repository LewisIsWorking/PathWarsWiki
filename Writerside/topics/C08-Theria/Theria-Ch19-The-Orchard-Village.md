## The Source of the White Plume

> Three days of walking carried the party out of the hill country and into a softer land of orchards and stone walls. The plume of white smoke turned out to be the chimney of a single house on a hill, and the village around it had no idea what had happened to its neighbour to the north.

>> 1. The trees were heavy with autumn fruit. The dirt paths between rows were swept. Children were working the lower branches under a few watchful adults.
>> 2. **Heron's** pull dimmed and steadied at the gate. He had been brought here on purpose.

## The Old Man

> The old man at the hilltop house was thin and small and had hands that had lived too many decades to count politely. He met the party at his porch with bread, butter, and an open expression.

>> 1. He did not give his name on the first asking. He gave it on the second. *"Call me Old, if titles bother you. Most do."*
>> 2. He had been expecting them. He did not say so. The bread was already cut for four.

## Húxiān, in Another Shape

> Húxiān stepped onto the porch behind the others and stopped at the door. The old man's eyes moved over her once and stayed for a moment too long.

>> 1. She shifted, with a small effort, into the human form she sometimes wore in towns. Short dark hair. No tail. Eyes the same two colours, harder to spot beneath a fringe.
>> 2. *"Forgive me,"* she said. *"I would rather not be a kitsune in your house until I know whether you would prefer one."*
>> 3. *"Either is welcome,"* the old man said, with a smile that meant it.

## Dura

> A woman in her thirties came up the hill from the orchard with a basket of apples and a small girl of perhaps eight on her hip. She introduced herself as **Dura**, the old man's daughter, and let the girl down to look at **Bereket** with the open astonishment small children reserve for centaurs.

>> 1. Dura had been the one who had seen the smoke from the north and sent her father a bird about it. *"He told me you would be here within a day. He has these moments."*
>> 2. **Quack** examined the apples and asked, professionally, whether the orchard had ever been used for distillation. Dura laughed. She had been planning to ask for a recipe.

## The Raid That Was Coming

> Over dinner, the old man laid out what he had not been able to put in a letter.

>> 1. The raiders that had taken the village to the north were not the only column. A second column had been seen on the southern road three days prior, smaller and faster, almost certainly coming this way.
>> 2. *"Two days, perhaps three, before they arrive. The village can be defended. It cannot be defended alone."*
>> 3. **Heron** looked at the old man for a long second. Whatever he saw, he kept to himself. *"We will help."*

> The old man poured more wine for everyone except Húxiān, who had asked for water.
