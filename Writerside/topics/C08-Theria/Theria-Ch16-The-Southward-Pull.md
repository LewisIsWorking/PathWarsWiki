## Following the Tug

> Heron's pull did not weaken on the road. It strengthened. He kept the heading south-southwest through the swamp's edge, across the hill country beyond, and along a low road that had not been on any map **Bereket** carried.

>> 1. **Húxiān** kept the conversation light at first and let Heron concentrate. By the third day she was asking Bereket about the orc slave master he had been hunting. By the fifth, **Quack** had started cataloguing field plants for medicinal uses out of pure professional reflex.
>> 2. The land grew drier. The trees thinned. The pull grew more insistent.

## Eight Days

> They counted. Eight days of steady walking, brief rests, careful watches. Nothing came at them on the road.

>> 1. Bereket noted that for a region known for its troubles, the silence was unnatural. Quack noted that the local fauna had clearly been moving away from something for some time.
>> 2. On the seventh evening, a wind from the south carried the smell of burnt wood.

## The Smoke

> The horizon on the eighth morning was wrong. A column of grey smoke standing nearly straight up in the windless air, a few miles south.

>> 1. Heron's pull pointed directly at it.
>> 2. The party did not need to discuss. They quickened their pace.
