## The Ritual Cavern

> Stone walkways ringed a dais of unfamiliar stone. Four crystals glowed and hummed above it. Beneath each crystal stood a cultist, chanting in a language none of the party recognised. The chant filled the air like pressure.

>> 1. Between the four chanters, a **dome of energy** pulsed in rhythm with the chant. Inside the dome, a **young girl** hunched in evident agony. Her eyes met the party's and she screamed without sound.
>> 2. Beyond the dais, a platform held a throne. A cultist there gripped a dimly glowing sword and shouted *"Do not let them stop the ritual! We are nearly finished!"*
>> 3. Four shielded swordsmen guarded the walkways. Two archers on each pathway, four total. More chanters stood within glyph circles on the floor. None looked away from their work.

## The Fight

> Seven rounds of pressure, smoke, and screams.

>> 1. **Waldemar** shoved one of the shielded swordsmen ten feet back with a critical strike. **Bereket** opened with an arrow into the east archer's heart. **Heron** taunted from the line and **Ali** struck where the gap opened.
>> 2. Mid-fight, the **leader's eyes flashed purple**. His voice changed. He moved with a confidence he had not had a moment before, and stepped in front of one of the chanters as if defending a piece of himself.
>> 3. **Heron** tested the barrier and was scorched for his trouble. The energy lashed out the closer he came. The girl inside curled tighter and looked at him through tears.
>> 4. The party cut through the shielded line and the archers in turn. **Waldemar** brought a chanter down with a maul shimmering in purple energy, gravity bent around the strike. **Ali** opened a second chanter's throat. **Bereket** put an arrow through the last archer.
>> 5. The leader staggered, bleeding heavily, and would not stop talking: *"Even if you bring me down, you cannot stop what is to come. You're too late."* **Waldemar** finished him with the haft of the maul to the face.

## The Pulse

> The chant did not stop when the cultists fell. The chanters who remained drove their daggers into their own chests and dropped onto the glyphs.

>> 1. Their blood pooled into the marks and the glyphs ignited with light. The dome around the girl exploded outward in a pulse that knocked the party to the ground.
>> 2. **Memories ran through every mind that were not their own**, but felt as if they belonged. Lifetimes settled in on top of lifetimes. Bodies ached. Blood burned. Something fundamental had changed.
>> 3. The girl was gone. A woman stood in her place: *"Can't say it's a form I'd have chosen, but it will do."*

## The Messenger Task

> She turned slowly and met each of their eyes the way an adult meets a child.

>> 1. *"You all did well in coming here,"* she said. *"I was hoping to end up with a messenger or so. Oh, I probably should apologize for that. Things are going to be different now. They are going to be what they always should have been."*
>> 2. She stopped in front of **Heron**, looked displeased for a moment, then let it pass. *"Go back to your people. Tell them that I am coming for the siblings. Tell them I will have my revenge."*
>> 3. She snapped her fingers and a pouch landed with a satisfying *plink* in the central circle. *"There's your compensation for this task."*
>> 4. She walked toward the passageway on the western side of the cavern. *"Nith M'aera is back. The era of magic has returned."*

> The party stood among the dead, four crystals dimming above them, and tried to remember which of their two memories had ever been real.
