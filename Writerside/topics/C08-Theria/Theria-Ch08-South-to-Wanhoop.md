## The Road South

> Bright hilly fields gave way to the southern lowlands. Food and water came easily on the way. **Leaf's** sense of direction held its end of the deal.

>> 1. The **Wanhoop Swamp** introduced itself with a sharp change of weather and smell. Dark canopy overhead, water under hoof and boot, and an odour that rose from the rot at distance.

## The Bridge

> A rocky bridge spanned a sunken pit of algae and rot. Standing on the bridge was a creature that did not quite resemble a chicken.

>> 1. Its wings were leathery. Its skin sloughed. It hissed and fanned itself at the party and prepared to charge. The **cockatrice** had not yet been named, but it would be.
>> 2. **Heron** placed himself between **Leaf** and the threat and raised his shield with a prayer to Dyrena. **Bereket** opened with an arrow into its side. **Leaf** drew a small bow and put a second arrow into the beast.
>> 3. The fight ran three rounds. The creature's beak found Heron's face on a critical strike near the eye, a peck that should have begun a slow turn to stone. Heron's body refused the petrification, his fortitude holding.
>> 4. **Leaf** flanked, drew their shortsword, and finished the cockatrice with two clean stabs through a vital organ. *"I am Leaf?"* they asked, looking at the blood on their hands.

## The Beast Named

> Sorting through their two lifetimes of memory, Heron and Bereket recognised the creature: a cockatrice, and a larger and hardier one than the stories tended to describe. Petrification through a peck was the famous trait.

>> 1. Heron checked his reflection in a nearby pool of water and found no stone in the wound on his cheek. He took a moment to thank **Dyrena** properly. Leaf pulled herbal bandages from a pouch beneath their cloak and patched the rest.
>> 2. **Bereket** scanned the area in case the beast had had a mate. None showed.

> Leaf pointed deeper into the swamp and led the way.
