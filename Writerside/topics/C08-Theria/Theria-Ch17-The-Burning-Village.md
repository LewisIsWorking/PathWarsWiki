# Chapter 17: The Burning Village

## Arrival

> The village had been small. Twenty buildings, give or take, in a shallow bowl between hills. Half of it was burning. Half of it was already done burning.

>> 1. **Heron's** pull eased the moment he crested the rise. Whatever had wanted him here, wanted him here.
>> 2. **Bereket** counted bodies first, by habit. **Quack** counted survivors. **Húxiān** counted attackers, and held up a finger to ask the party to wait.

## The First Wave

> Six orc raiders moving through the southern half of the village in a loose pattern. They had not yet noticed the new arrivals.

>> 1. **Bereket** opened with a long arrow that took the rear orc through the throat. The rest of the wave turned and charged at once.
>> 2. **Heron** held the centre of the line with shield up, **Quack** flanking with a drawn-out claw mutagen. **Húxiān** put two healing pulses into the line as it formed.
>> 3. The wave broke under sustained fire. The last orc tried to fall back south and met an arrow in the back at sixty paces.

## A Survivor

> A small voice from beneath what had been a barn. Quack moved the beam off, gently. Beneath was a **halfling** in his middle years, soot-streaked, eyes ringed from crying.

>> 1. **Wrenwick Willowberry**, the village's apothecary. His wife was somewhere in the rubble two streets over. His own child, an infant, was with her. Neither was alive.
>> 2. He pressed something into Heron's hand: three small vials of prepared infant formula in stoppered glass. *"For Samara. Not mine. Samara's. Sue and Hal's baby. Sue and Hal are gone. Samuel and Samson are gone. Samara is in the cellar of the church, if the church still stands. Please."*
>> 3. Heron took the vials.

## The Second Wave

> A horn cut across the village. Larger than the first, from the southern road.

>> 1. The second wave came in tight formation behind a single orc in heavier armour, helm crested, axe with a cruel curve. A **herald**. Eight raiders with him.
>> 2. The herald spoke first. *"Burning the village was the message. The next message comes for those who try to put out the fire."*
>> 3. **Heron** answered in **Bereket's** voice. *"Then come and burn us."*
>> 4. The fight ran long. Bereket focused the herald, four arrows landing in succession before the orc closed the distance. Quack mutagen-up and met the herald's axe with a clawed forearm and held. Húxiān put healing into the line on a rhythm. Heron broke the wave by tripping the centre orc, opening the line, and Bereket finished the herald with an arrow through the eye-slit.
>> 5. The remaining raiders fell or fled.

## What the Herald Carried

> The herald's gear was beyond what a raider should have carried.

>> 1. A **runic-etched breastplate**, faintly magical and well-fitted. An **Orc Necksplitter** axe with a clean balance. Two **lesser Elixirs of Life** stoppered in iron. A small selection of alchemical vials, mostly intact.
>> 2. Quack identified the alchemy. Bereket took the axe. Heron took the breastplate. Húxiān took an Elixir for the kit and left the other for the party.

## Samara

> The church still stood, more or less. The cellar door was still locked from the inside. The party knocked, called Wrenwick's name as a passphrase, and the door opened on an old woman holding a wailing infant in her arms.

>> 1. **Samara**. Roughly six months old. Hungry.
>> 2. Heron handed the old woman the three vials of formula. She wept and could not stop weeping. Quack gave her a tonic for steadier hands.
