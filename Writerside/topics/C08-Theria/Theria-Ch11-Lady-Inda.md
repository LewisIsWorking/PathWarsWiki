## The Cabin

> The cabin was older than it had any business being, with herbs hanging in bundles from the porch beams and a roof that the moss had treated as a tenant. **Lady Inda** stood in the doorway with arms crossed, a grey-haired woman in faded green skirts who did not bother to smile when she saw the party.

>> 1. *"Húxiān. I see you brought half the swamp with you."*
>> 2. Húxiān gave her mother the polite tilt of head one gives a very particular kind of relative. *"They need a healer, Mother. The kind that knows what a curse smells like."*
>> 3. Inda's nose wrinkled once and stayed wrinkled. She gestured them in.

## Curses Sniffed

> The inside of the cabin was tidier than the outside suggested. Herbs everywhere. Two cauldrons, one bubbling. A fox-shaped lamp on the table. A book on a stand that closed itself politely when the party entered.

>> 1. Inda took the party in one at a time. **Heron** carried the faint singe of a goddess's attention on him. **Bereket** carried something older that had folded under the pulse and not yet shaken loose. **Quack** carried nothing worth her time, in her words. **Leaf** she stared at for a full minute and would not explain.
>> 2. She moved a finger over Bereket's spine and said something in a language none of them recognised. Whatever had been folded inside him unfolded again. He thanked her stiffly.
>> 3. She would not, she said, touch what was on Heron. *"That is between you and her."*

## The Trade

> Inda agreed to walk back to the village with them and deal with the Jinkin curse personally. In exchange, the party would fetch her a flower.

>> 1. *"An **Atrix Blossom**. There is only one place it still grows. The **Twilight Ruins**, three days east. It will be the only thing alive in there."*
>> 2. She would not say more about why she needed it. *"I have my reasons, and you have your village. Do not be slow."*
>> 3. Inda gave **Heron** a sealed clay jar to carry the blossom in. The jar was lined with cool wet moss and a sprig of something silvered. *"The blossom dies easily. Keep the jar shut until you are back to me."*

## A Few Honest Words About the World

> Heron asked, on their way out, whether Inda believed Nith was kin to the Seven.

>> 1. Inda laughed without humour. *"No. She has spent millennia pretending she is, and the Seven have spent millennia pretending it is not so. They fear her. Especially the boy."*
>> 2. She did not explain which boy. She closed the door behind them.

## Húxiān Comes Along

> Húxiān adjusted her pack and fell in step with the party.

>> 1. *"Mother is in a mood. The Twilight Ruins are not a place for travellers, and she would prefer I make sure you come back alive enough to be useful."*
>> 2. **Leaf** pointed east and said, with confidence, *"I am Leaf!"*
