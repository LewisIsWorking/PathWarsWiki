## What the Pulse Left Behind

> Every member of the party carried a second life now, layered against the first.

>> 1. **Ali** had died as a human child to an over-zealous man from the mainland. A power she had worshipped in both timelines refused to let her stay dead. She wore the form of a ghost now.
>> 2. **Bereket** had moved from two legs to four. He stood as a centaur, hooves on the cave floor, and the muscle memory of standing as a foal came to him as easily as the memory of being a man.
>> 3. **Waldemar** had been cursed by cultists in both timelines. In the old world he had suppressed the curse and lived as a man. In this one the curse had taken form, with enhanced senses and eyes that turned animal in combat.
>> 4. **Heron** carried two memories and one body, and would later realise the world had quietly added new shapes to it.

## A Survivor

> Hidden in a side chamber off the ritual cavern was an elf the cult had intended to use next.

>> 1. **Silas Dimroot** introduced himself with the small panic of someone who suspected information was the only thing keeping him alive. He did not know what the cult wanted, only that he was meant to be sacrificed.
>> 2. The party shared the antivenom and a shortsword, then took him with them.

## The Pit, Revisited

> Climbing back through the cave, the party passed the spike pit again.

>> 1. The body still lay impaled at the bottom, but it was no longer Brother Elias as they had known him. In this timeline, the lieutenant had been a **dwarf**, and the dwarf had still found the pit.
>> 2. The lieutenant's glowing orb of light still hung above his corpse.

## Reunion with Captain Kael

> Voices and commotion at the entry shaft above announced the rest of the search party arriving.

>> 1. About thirty soldiers waited above, in a mix of races that would have been impossible to imagine a day before. Humans, elves, dwarves, halflings, gnomes, and even goblins, all in familiar uniform.
>> 2. **Captain Valerius Kael** himself had become an **orc** in this timeline. His anger was at himself, not at the party, for being too late to stop the ritual.
>> 3. The party told him what they knew: the ritual had succeeded, an entity calling herself **Nith M'aera** had taken the body of a woman, and she had charged them with a message about coming for "the siblings."
>> 4. As the captain began to reply, his body stiffened. His eyes glowed a bright blue. A voice not his own spoke: *"Return to the capital immediately. College of Cumeyr. We have much to discuss."*
>> 5. The eyes faded. The captain shook off the moment without comment. He gave the order to return.

## Domusirex

> A goblin chanted, drew shapes in the air with his fingers, and opened a portal. The captain stepped through, and the soldiers followed.

>> 1. The portal placed the party in the **military district of Domusirex**, the capital of **Asmarintine** and the largest city in the known lands. A teleportation platform, carved for the purpose, sat under their hooves and boots.
>> 2. The same goblin who had opened the portal placed a small magical sigil on each of their hands. The captain explained: do not leave the city until they were called for, but they were welcome to the walls.
>> 3. He told them the upper brass would want to question them. *"It may take a few days for everyone to arrive."* A flicker of compassion crossed his stern face. *"May the twins make your time here comfortable and rewarding."*
>> 4. The quartermaster paid each of them 15 gold. The party was on a leash of about a week, in a city that had not existed in their first memory of the world.
