## A Village in Slow Decay

> Leaf brought the party to a mid-sized village that had clearly lost a fight with its own ground. Sixty mud-stained buildings, walls cracked, sections collapsed, faces in windows that looked at broken stalls more than at the strangers walking through.

>> 1. The village had sunk into the swamp some years prior, but the damage on display today was newer and stranger. Tools snapped. Walls split. Things broke without reason.

## The Inn

> The inn was in disarray but kept its warmth. The innkeeper, a bald dwarven man with a hefty grey beard and skin the colour of pale ash, kept a wry tone and an honest price list.

>> 1. Two copper for the stable, seven copper per bed, four silver for a private room with a busted lock. One copper per meal if prepaid; two if not.
>> 2. There was already a guest in the dining area.

## Quacksalver

> The guest was a hobgoblin. Steely skin under the lamp light, vials open on the table in front of him. He greeted **Bereket** with the careful warmth of someone who knew his species made an impression.

>> 1. **Quacksalver**, *chemist with intent to dispense*, was in the marshes researching local flora for its medicinal value. He offered alchemy: healing elixirs, tonics for blows, the occasional flask of fire, and a selection of mutagens for tailoring talent to circumstance.
>> 2. **Heron**, fresh from watching **Ali** become a ghost and **Waldemar** become whatever Waldemar had become, refused the mutagens with feeling.
>> 3. To allay Heron's panic, Quack drank his own demonstration vial. His arms stretched rubbery and grew until he could shake hands at ten feet. The effect would wear off in minutes.

## The Curse on the Village

> An audible crack from across the room: the innkeeper holding a broom now broken in two. Second of the month.

>> 1. The damage had been escalating for two weeks. Roughly the same window since **Nith M'aera's** release. **Bereket** cast *detect magic* and found faint magic on every broken object in the inn.
>> 2. Morning found the party's own gear afflicted. **Heron's** shield strap kept failing to hold, **Bereket's** bow had warped, **Leaf's** thieves' tools jangled in a now-loose pouch, and **Quack's** alchemy kit had holes that let reagents slip between pockets.

## The Jinkins

> **Bereket** spotted them: small bipedal creatures with disproportionately large heads, ducking behind buildings.

>> 1. He and Heron sorted their memories. **Jinkins.** Spiteful fey on their own; in a group, capable of cursing objects into unreliable shapes.
>> 2. The trail led to an abandoned home on the edge of the village. The Jinkins had set themselves up for an ambush, but the party had already noticed them, and the ambush turned around.

## The Fight

> Six Jinkins. The party held initiative.

>> 1. **Heron** held the line with shielded taunts and trips. **Leaf** flanked and danced between Jinkins, dropping one with a critical stab and a second with a flanked strike. **Quack** healed **Bereket** through the relentless poking the centaur took on his legs.
>> 2. **Bereket** used *timber* and arrows to clear angles. **Heron** drank a potion mid-fight when his cheek bled into his mouth.
>> 3. The Jinkins fell one by one. The last two pressed Bereket hard, opening shallow but accumulating wounds. By the fourth round, four of six were down, and the rest broke under continued pressure.

> The remaining villagers came out as the noise died. The innkeeper would later admit he could only offer the party a free meal as thanks. He had a name to recommend instead.
