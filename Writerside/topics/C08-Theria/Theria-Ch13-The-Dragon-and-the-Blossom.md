## A Clearing for Leaf

> On the first morning of the walk back, **Húxiān** asked the party to stop. There was a clearing the swamp had let her remember, a piece of dry ground with old trees still standing.

>> 1. They dug a shallow grave with the small spades **Quack** had been carrying for botanical samples. **Heron** said a short prayer for Leaf as a thing made of grace. **Bereket** stood guard at the edge of the clearing.
>> 2. They planted Leaf's body in the dirt and covered them over. There was no marker.

## The Dragon

> The growl that followed had the polite warning of a creature that thought of itself as territorial rather than hungry.

>> 1. A **young green dragon** descended through the canopy at the edge of the clearing, low and sinewy, scales the colour of wet moss and a smile too wide for an animal.
>> 2. It addressed them in a draconic that **Húxiān** translated for the others. It wanted to know who had buried a thing of its mother's making on its land.
>> 3. *"Your mother is a goddess?"* Heron asked.
>> 4. *"My mother is a hag,"* the dragon said, in slightly accented Common. *"You should have asked permission."*

## The Fight

> The dragon meant the lesson, not the kill. The party would learn this only after the fight had run long enough to be unsure.

>> 1. **Bereket** opened with two arrows into the dragon's wing. **Heron** placed himself between the dragon and Húxiān and weathered a sweep of the tail with a shield that nearly buckled. **Quack** drank a mutagen that sent his claws longer than they had been.
>> 2. The dragon's breath weapon caught **Bereket** in a long cone of acidic mist and dropped his hindquarters into a stagger. Quack hauled him out of the cloud and emptied an elixir into him.
>> 3. The dragon let the fight tilt. Its blood was running. It accepted the lesson it had wanted to teach in reverse, and lifted, slow and bleeding, into the canopy.
>> 4. *"My mother will hear about this,"* it called down, and was gone.

## The Blossom

> Húxiān was the first to notice. The grave they had dug for Leaf had a stem rising from it now, slender and silvered, with a single bud at the top.

>> 1. The bud opened slowly while they watched. White petals, gold at the centre, a faint warmth that the swamp around them seemed to lean toward.
>> 2. **Heron** opened the clay jar Inda had given him. The cool wet moss was still cool and wet, the silvered sprig still silvered. He cut the stem with a careful blade and placed the **Atrix Blossom** inside.
>> 3. The jar sealed itself the moment the lid touched it.

## West Again

> The party stood at Leaf's grave for a longer moment than any of them wanted to admit, and then turned west again.

>> 1. **Húxiān** kept the pace. The swamp let them through.
