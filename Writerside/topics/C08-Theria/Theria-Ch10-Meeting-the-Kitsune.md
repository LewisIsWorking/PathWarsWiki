## A Name and a Guide

> The innkeeper would not let the party leave without a name. The closest help to the Jinkin curse, in his view, was a grumpy old healer who lived deep in the swamp. **Lady Inda.** She did not enjoy visitors. The swamp did not enjoy strangers.

>> 1. He had a friend who could take them, though, if they were willing to wait the night. *"She drops by every now and then. I'll send a bird at first light."*
>> 2. By the next morning, the bird had returned with a small folded note pinned to its foot and a fox-eared traveller waiting outside the inn.

## Húxiān

> She was a mid-height **kitsune** in dyed leathers, with a fluffy red tail and the kind of cheer that only ever forms in spite of something.

>> 1. **Húxiān** introduced herself with a sectoral heterochromia that the lamplight made unsettling, one eye turquoise and the other a crystal blue. A wand of healing rode on her belt next to a short blade.
>> 2. Her humour was dry and edged. Her family had come, generations ago, from the kitsune empire of **Hwa-Lun**, and most of them had been *skinned by superstitious psychopathic aristocrats* before they had managed to get out. Jokes about poor skinning technique came easily.
>> 3. She knew the path to Lady Inda's cabin. She would take them on for the price of a meal at the inn on their return.

## The Swamp's Mood

> Húxiān led the party west and south, off the main path, through wetland the swamp seemed to prefer they not cross.

>> 1. She kept the pace conversational. She asked **Heron** about Domusirex, asked **Bereket** about Cumeyr's Rest, and gave **Quack** a pleased grin for the rubber-arm demonstration she had clearly heard about from the innkeeper.
>> 2. **Leaf** liked her. Leaf had not liked many people on the journey so far.

## Stranger Things

> Two days deeper into the swamp, the canopy darkened and the noise of insects fell away in a strange band that ran roughly east to west, as though the place had a curtain drawn across it.

>> 1. Húxiān walked through it without ceremony. *"Mother's edge,"* she said, as if naming a fence post.
>> 2. The first sign of habitation was a small bridge of moss-covered planks crossing a slow black channel, and beyond it, a cabin shape behind moss-hung trees.

> Húxiān whistled twice. A pause. A return whistle from the cabin.
