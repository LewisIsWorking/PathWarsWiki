## A Pause to Compare Notes

> Before splitting up to enjoy a week of forced leisure in the capital, **Heron** pulled the party aside and proposed an evening at a quiet tavern. They needed to sort out their double memories and align a story before the brass put questions to them.

## The Garden View

> The Garden View was a small tavern with a generous outdoor seating area beneath an awning woven from greenery. Jars of softly glowing rocks hung above the tables, and the air smelled of honey and apples. An elvish woman served the off-duty guardsmen and waved the party to a table when they approached.

>> 1. **Bereket** asked for the standard ale by the pitcher. He and **Heron** took bowls of stew with rosemary-salt bread. **Ali** ghostified a mug to drink from with some practice, and skipped the food.
>> 2. The waitress took the centaur and the half-corporeal woman in stride. She had seen enough variety in Domusirex to keep her professional voice.

## Backstories Laid Out

> Each of them told the others what little they could be sure was theirs.

>> 1. **Heron** had come south chasing coin for medicine. His mother was sick in his old memory, and he had no way to know whether she was still sick in this one, or whether she existed at all.
>> 2. **Bereket** had been away from his tribe for years before any of this, hunting an orcish slave master who had taken his sister and others from his people.
>> 3. **Ali** had died as a human child to an over-zealous man from the mainland. A higher power had refused to let her stay dead, and that same power was still hers to worship in both timelines. The man would be paid back.
>> 4. **Waldemar** carried the same curse in both worlds. In one he had suppressed it. In this one it had been allowed to take form.

## A Rumour, A Question, A Denial

> Bereket asked the waitress, gently, whether any story she knew told of the gods warring among themselves and imprisoning one of their own.

>> 1. The waitress thought about it carefully and said no. She had never heard a tale like that. *"As far as I know, the siblings have always gotten along with one another."*
>> 2. She did mention what the city was talking about. The **College** had been in some kind of agitation. The city guard had run errands at its direction the day before, and today had been told to stay away.

> The party did not push further. They paid their bill and walked to an inn for the night.

## The Speaking Birds

> They woke to birdsong outside the inn windows, and one by one realised the songs had words in them.

>> 1. The birds were speaking Common. They were speaking to the party. The phrase came simply and repeatedly: *"Come. Follow."*
