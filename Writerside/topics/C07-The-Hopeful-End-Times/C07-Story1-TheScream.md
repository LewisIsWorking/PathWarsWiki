# Story 1: The Scream

> The Scream is the first story of the Hopeful End-Times campaign.
>> - Started at Level 1.
>> - Part 1 is complete. Part 2 is in progress.

## Part 1: Ghost Levels

> The party accepted a salvage job from Julz, a vesk pawnbroker operating out of the Vesk Quarter.
>> - Julz had acquired a freighter at auction, rumoured to have been towed in from Azlanti space.
>> - He needed a crew to clear it out, catalogue the assets, and neutralise any dangers inside.
>> - The pay was a cut of the salvage profits.

> The party took a lift down toward the docks.
>> - Midway through the descent, a psychic shockwave tore through Absalom Station.
>> - Every comm unit screamed. The lights blazed to blinding intensity.
>> - For a split second, the image of a floating, tentacled monstrosity seared itself into view.
>> - Then everything went dark.
>> - The elevator plummeted.

> The party emerged not into the station they knew, but into the Ghost Levels.
>> - An extradimensional space hidden alongside Absalom Station.
>> - Steel corridors, 40-foot ceilings, catwalks, and auto-doors that opened and closed on proximity sensors.
>> - Comm units were dead. No signals in or out.
>> - The place was full of anomalies, dangerous creatures, and the remnants of people who had been pulled in by the same event.

> The party navigated the Ghost Levels, encountering hostile creatures and environmental hazards.
>> - A group of tourists had also been pulled in, along with their skittermander tour guide.

> During a combat encounter, a portal opened beneath Lysae's feet.
>> - Lysae fell through.
>> - As she passed through, her body transformed into something else entirely, a slime creature, outside the walls of an impossibly tall city.
>> - The portal closed immediately.
>> - The echoing sound of the psychic scream rang through the space where she had stood.
>> - Lysae was gone.

> The party eventually found a working lift and powered it up.
>> - The doors opened with a pleasant chime.
>> - They rode it back up into Absalom Station proper, emerging in the maintenance corridors beneath a neighbourhood called Little Akiton.

> Part 1 was completed on 2025-08-10.

## Part 2: Big Trouble in Little Akiton

> The party emerged into chaos.
>> - Emergency alerts flooded every comm unit. 
>> - The Starstone reactor had experienced an unexplained power flicker.
>> - Station-wide shelter-in-place orders were in effect.
>> - Security squads in riot gear patrolled corridors. Looters dashed through alleyways. News feeds broadcast gridlocked intersections and smashed storefronts.
>> - Little Akiton was cut off from the rest of the station by security roadblocks.

> Little Akiton is different from the rest of the station.
>> - A small, run-down neighbourhood built from repurposed shipping containers and old dock infrastructure.
>> - Its residents, mostly working-class immigrants from Akiton, are united by hardship and shared culture.
>> - In times of trouble, they band together. Shops share generators. Neighbours pool resources. Local businesses offer free food and shelter.

> The party also visited Castle-Counting, a timepiece and arms shop run by Ophelius Berrus.
>> - Castle-Counting sits in a cramped storefront in Little Akiton, packed with clocks of every era and make.
>> - Ophelius is an older human man with a mechanical multi-tool arm and a deep knowledge of both weapons and temporal mechanics.
>> - He recognised something in the party. Something the Scream had done to them.
>> - He catalogued each of them quietly, noting the way reality bent around certain individuals.
>> - Before the party left, Ophelius gave each member a watch. Different styles, different eras. No explanation offered.

> New members joined the party during the Little Akiton stay.
>> - Lowda Medina, a 14-foot Sarcesian from the Diaspora. He sees things others don't: ghosts, warp fields, layers of reality peeling apart. Pulse #423, a floating cloud of gemstones, orbits his head at all times.
>> - Rune, a 13-year-old deaf vlaka courier. An AbadarCorp employee by day, a rave kid by night. He communicates through sign language, a telepathy node, and comm unit text-to-speech.

> After completing a basement cleaning job, Manee offered the party another task.
>> - A shipment she was expecting had been held up at the docks.
>> - All the party had to do was go down to Dock 3, sign for the cargo, and bring it back.
>> - Manee warned them to be careful given the state of the station.

> The party descended to the Lower Shipyards.
>> - A boneyard of hulls, engine blocks, and half-gutted starships from a hundred years of Pact Worlds engineering.
>> - Six docking bays connected to ship-sized tunnels leading out of the station.

> Dock 3 was not the simple pickup Manee described.
>> - A shobhad dockworker named Tego lay unconscious on the walkway, laser pistol dropped inches from his fingers.
>> - A scuffed cargo shuttle sat at scaffold level, hatch open.
>> - Three station-born goblins had taken defensive positions: two gunners with rotolasers behind cargo containers, and their boss, Spicy Sweet, leaning out the shuttle hatch with a missile launcher on her shoulder.
>> - They had planted landmines along the walkway.

> The party defused the situation without bloodshed.
>> - Livy hit her signature IDOL POSE at gunpoint, which was stupid, brave, and somehow effective.
>> - Lowda named Manee, named Tego, and kept his hands visible. Calm. Practical.
>> - Spicy Sweet explained: they were delivery crew. Tego saw goblins and drew first. The goblins stunned him and dug in.
>> - Alita stabilised Tego with a combat stim.
>> - Chua helped Grikk disarm the mines.
>> - The cargo manifest checked out: food, parts, and medical supplies. Nothing illegal.
>> - Spicy Sweet demanded someone sign for the shipment and told them to tell Manee she owes hazard pay.

> The party is currently at Dock 3 preparing to transport the cargo back to Manee Moonpuncher.
