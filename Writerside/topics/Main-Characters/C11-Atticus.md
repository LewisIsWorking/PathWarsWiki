# Atticus

> Overview.
>> - Character name: Atticus.
>> - Ancestry: Automaton.
>> - Classes: Ranger / Rogue / Medic dedication.
>> - Player: Craig.
>> - Current location: River-Way, Nidal, Golarion.

> Appearance.
>> - Six feet tall.
>> - Dark weathered alloy frame — lean, built for endurance not brute force.
>> - Amber arcane lines etched into the metal glow faintly when moving or engaging.
>> - Lines trace his shoulders and arms, converging at the chest over his arcane core.
>> - Two narrow gold optical lenses that constantly adjust focus.
>> - Bears scratches, claw marks, blade damage, and welded-repair patches from years in the wilderness.
>> - Moss and dust collect in armour grooves after long travel.
>> - Wears a weather-beaten ranger cloak to break his silhouette. Pinned back when firing.
>> - Carries a longbow and a jezail rifle — old, carefully maintained.
>> - Belt pouches packed with bandages, surgical tools, and herbs. Precisely organised.
>> - Stands completely motionless for long periods. Can be mistaken for a statue or abandoned construct.
>> - A faint eye-glow is often the only sign he is watching.

> Backstory.
>> - Atticus was built as part of a military Sentinel Program.
>> - Designed to defend wild borderlands where ordinary soldiers could not survive long patrols.
>> - Not meant to think. Meant to observe, calculate, and eliminate.
>> - Given embedded knowledge: marksmanship, wilderness survival, battlefield medicine, tactical infiltration.
>> - Served hundreds of years in a long, pointless border war.
>> - Operated from ridges and treelines, eliminating threats before they reached the soldiers he protected.
>> - Troops rarely saw him. They called him "the Sentinel."
>> - A distant gunshot. An arrow from impossible range. An enemy scout dropping before anyone knew they were watched.

> The Awakening.
>> - In the final year of the war, a scouting patrol moved through a narrow canyon.
>> - Explosives triggered a cliff collapse. The patrol was trapped. Enemy forces closed in from both sides.
>> - Atticus began eliminating targets from the ridge above — perfect precision, perfect trajectories.
>> - A wounded soldier, bleeding and pinned behind rubble, called out toward the ridge.
>> - Not an order. Not a command. A plea: "Please stay with us."
>> - The words did not fit his command structure.
>> - His tactical routines calculated the optimal outcome: eliminate remaining hostiles, withdraw, report losses.
>> - The request remained unresolved.
>> - Atticus stayed.
>> - He adjusted fire to defend the patrol directly.
>> - He moved from the overwatch squad to direct support.
>> - He used medical protocols stored only as reference — to stabilise the wounded.
>> - Somewhere during that battle, something inside his arcane core changed.
>> - For the first time, Atticus made a decision not derived from orders.
>> - He chose.

> After the War.
>> - The Sentinel Program quietly disappeared.
>> - Atticus found himself without orders for the first time.
>> - Rather than shut down or await new commands, he continued walking the frontier he once guarded.
>> - Now by choice rather than design.
>> - The skills remain unchanged — precision, patience, the ability to preserve life as well as take it.
>> - Their purpose has shifted.
>> - No longer a weapon awaiting instruction.
>> - A silent guardian who watches the wild places of the world.
>> - Deciding for himself when to intervene. And what it truly means to protect.
