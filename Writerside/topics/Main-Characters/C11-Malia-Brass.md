> Backstory.
>> - In the heart of the Blistering Glade.
>> - A forest so hot the dew sizzles at dawn.
>> - Malia Brass was born from two equally questionable life choices.
>> - Her mother, a whimsical sprite named Floria Dewstep, fell in love with a mysterious stranger who wore too much cologne and smelled faintly of brimstone.
>> - That stranger turned out to be a Diabolic Fire Genie from the city of Brass (hence Malia's last name).
>> - Nine months later, Malia emerged from a sunflower that had spontaneously combusted, giggling like a coal in the wind.
>> - Growing up wasn't easy when your lullaby was a demonic laugh echoing through your dreams and your bathwater sometimes boiled itself.
>> - The other sprites nicknamed her "Matchstick," half out of affection, half because she'd accidentally set the Elder Tree on fire — twice.
>> - But Malia was built differently, literally glowing with sparks whenever she danced beneath the sun.
>> - Her talent landed her among the Sun Dancers, a troupe of elemental artists who believed rhythm could awaken the spirits of fire itself. (They were right; Malia's dance once caused a volcano to sneeze.)
>> - Everything changed when she met Iron, a fetching with soot in his veins and eyes like banked embers — a creature both forged and forsaken, halfway between mortal and elemental.
>> - A brooding blacksmith with arms like ironwood and a laugh like thunder, Iron worked the forge as though it were a cathedral.
>> - Their romance burned hot and bright, the kind of love that leaves burns even when you're careful.
>> - Then, one day, he was gone — leaving behind only the brass pendant he'd forged for her, a piece that hums faintly when she dreams.
>> - Some say the same shadows that whisper her father's name took him.
>> - Others say Iron simply couldn't bear the heat she carried.
>> - Now, Malia walks the roads of Golarion, her path having led her — for the moment — to the town of River Way in shadowed Nidal.
>> - She performs when the mood takes her, light carving through darkness, her fire a rebellion against the quiet.
>> - Pedro, her fiery corgi companion, trots at her heel, leaving small scorch prints in the cobblestones, ever eager to taste trouble (especially if it smells like bacon).
>> - She smiles often. It's the kind of smile that makes candles flicker, gossip pause midair, and gods quietly check their fire alarms.
