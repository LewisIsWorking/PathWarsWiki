## Overview

The Temple of the Sun is an ancient building in the southern end of the Mozimba Camp neighbourhood of Kibwe. 

The party must reach it so that **Eda Stone-Worth The Curse-Breaker** can complete her ritual.

According to Tal'lysae, the Temple is currently held by people "who care nothing for Kibwe's survival but their own shallow desires." 

She specifically mentioned **Paga Nikohian** as being in the Sun Temple.

---

## What the Party Knows

- Eda/Cursebreaker has sprinted ahead and is likely already at the Temple.
- The Temple contains ancient magic connected to the Pillar-Watchers.
- Curse-breaker must reach the Temple to address the slithering curse.
- Someone named Paga Nikohian may have accelerated the curse.
- The curse is evolving and may soon affect non-human species.
- Nexian mages across the city are running rituals to slow the curse but are near collapse.

## Session Log

### The White Flame Merchant Slime

On a wide merchant street, the party witnessed a human merchant beating his triceratops.
On the third blow, the merchant transformed into a sewer ooze.

A Taralu dwarf cleric named Bashevis leapt to calm the dinosaur but was impaled on its horn.
The merchant slime absorbed the dying dwarf's divine spirit and evolved into the White Flame Merchant Slime.

The party engaged the creature across two brutal rounds of combat.
The slime engulfed Daichi Kenshin and Kieran.
Daichi was forced through dimensional rifts inside the slime's body and nearly died.
Tarsus counterspelled the slime's teleportation.

In Round 2, Tal'lysae aided Fierce Leopard's assault.
When the slime attempted to teleport again, Tal'lysae screamed for Fierce Leopard to step into the portal.
He did.
The slime exploded.

Fierce Leopard vanished.

Only seconds later, he returned in a burst of white light.
On his hands and knees.
Covered in slime, blood, and vomit.
Shaking.

Fierce Leopard has not yet shared what he saw.

### Kieran and Dimitri

After the battle, Kieran attempted to shoo the triceratops away.
It refused to leave.
The dinosaur pressed its painted frill gently against Kieran's chest and fell into step behind him.

Kieran named it Dimitri.
He wiped away the garish crimson and gold paint from its frill.
Eda settled on top of the dinosaur as a guardian.

Eda absorbed residual spatial slime from the White Flame encounter and grew in power.
When Kieran's slime touched the triceratops, Eda's slime sank into it and glowed green momentarily.

### Master Cho Kobo

A tengu monk approached the party after the slime battle, offering his assistance.
Kieran welcomed him.

### The Walk to the Temple

Ademe suggested that she, Dhira, and Tavik guard the temple entrance rather than go inside.
She also advised against bringing Theron into the temple.

Tal'lysae and Lorn had an emotional conversation during the walk.
Tal'lysae revealed that the flame saved her but did not save her freedom.
She expressed jealousy of Lorn's freedom and sadness that his destiny now lies elsewhere.
Lorn defended Selenor as family, not a slave-master, and expressed belief that his father can be saved.

### The Watchtower

The party reached a stone watchtower where dozens of Nexian mages and other casters were running a ritual to slow the curse.
Purple lightning crackled from the roof.
A green orb kept manifesting and being popped back out of reality.
The mages were near collapse from exhaustion.

Tavik spoke with city guards at the base and learned the following:
- The curse is evolving.
- If not stopped at the temple, it will mutate to affect all non-halfling species.
- The mages have less than an hour before they collapse entirely.

Theron revealed that Paga may be specifically targeting gnomes, dwarves, and similar races.
Dhira Graycheeks pulled a gun on Theron for withholding this information.

Ademe pointed at Kieran and connected the curse's origins to a slime god's holy book from thousands of years ago.

The party now faces a choice: assist the failing ritual or push on to the temple.

**Tavik argues to stay.** Save the people. Help the mages.

**Ademe argues to go.** Time is short. If the party dies at the temple, the curse mutates anyway.

**Tal'lysae remains silent.**

**Moffat** (the Nexian envoy from Chapter 1) is among the mages at the top of the tower.

### The Ritual (If the Party Helps)

The ritual requires the following skills from different characters:
- Occultism (or Arcana at a higher DC)
- Medicine
- Kibwe Lore
- Slime Lore

Dhira explained that if the party takes over as primary casters, the difficulty increases but the timeframe shortens considerably.

### Current Status

The party is deciding. Responses so far:
- Kieran agrees with Ademe but wants to find a way to help both.
- Master Cho Kobo suggests heading to the temple may reveal insights.
- Lorn: "I am not a mage. I just shoot my bow."
- Tarsus offered to stay and help with the ritual solo while others proceed to the temple.
